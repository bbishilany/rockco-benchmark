Why?

Three legs of organic growth

Your serious competitors are growing on three legs. Rockefeller is running on two.

⇒

Referrals

The primary growth engine for most firms. Existing clients refer friends, family, colleagues. It works, but it doesn't scale predictably and you can't control the volume. When referrals slow down, there's no lever to pull.

↔

Centers of Influence

CPAs, estate attorneys, insurance professionals who send clients your way. Strong COI relationships take years to build and depend on individual people. When a key COI retires or moves, that pipeline goes with them.

↑

SEO / AIO

The firms winning market share right now are the ones who built this. Creative Planning, Mariner Wealth, Hightower — they all have location pages, service content, and directory infrastructure in every market they serve. When someone in Dallas searches "wealth management firm near me," those firms show up. Firms without that infrastructure don't exist in that search. This is the only leg that compounds over time without adding headcount.

Referrals and COI relationships are working for Rockefeller. The third leg isn't. This benchmark measures the size of that gap across 5 markets, and what it would take to build the organic infrastructure that turns 140+ offices into search assets.

What Location Architecture Enables

A proper location hierarchy does three things that flat team pages cannot:

1. Indexability — Google can discover and rank dedicated /city/ pages for “financial advisor [city]” queries. Team bios don’t target those terms.

2. Internal link equity — State rollup pages pass authority to city pages, which pass to individual advisor profiles. This compounds over time.

3. Service+City combinations — The highest-converting keywords pair a service with a city (“wealth management Dallas”). Without dedicated pages, there’s nothing to rank.
